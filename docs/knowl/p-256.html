Let \(X\) be a smooth projective curve over \(K\) a number field and let \(Z\) be a 0-dimensional subscheme. Let \(U = X-Z\text{,}\) \(d= \#Z(\overline K)\text{.}\) The topological Euler characteristic of \(U\) is \(\chi (U) = \chi (X) - d = 2g-2 -d\text{.}\) If \(\chi (U) \lt 0\) we say that \(U \) is hyperbolic (we want to consider hyperbolic curves because they have nonabelian \(\pi _1\text{.}\))
