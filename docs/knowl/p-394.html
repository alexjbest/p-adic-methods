Suppose \(J\) is absolutely simple, and let \(Z\in \Pic(X\times X)\) a symmetric correspondence. Then the class associated to \(Z\) lies in the subspace

\begin{equation*} \bigwedge^2 H^1(X)(1)\subseteq H^1(X)\otimes H^1(X)(1)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Moreover \(Z\) is nice iff the image of this class in \(H^2(X)(1)\) under the cup product is zero.
