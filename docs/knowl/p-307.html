\(\pi _1^\dR\) is equipped with a Hodge filtration. Just like we took the lower central series filtration on \(\pi _1^\et(X_{\overline K})_{\QQ_p}\text{.}\) do the same thing for quotients by

\begin{equation*} \pi _1^\dR(X_{K_p})_n \end{equation*}

We'll denote these as \(U_n^\dR\text{.}\) We have

\begin{equation*} U_n^\dR = D_{cris}(U_n^\et) \end{equation*}

so it is equipped with a frobenius action.
