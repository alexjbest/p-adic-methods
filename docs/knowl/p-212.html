Back to \(E_2 (E,\omega )\text{,}\) Katz '73, gave an interpretation of \(E_2(E,\omega )\) as a direction of the unit-root eigenspace of frobenius acting on Monsky-Washnitzer cohomology. Suppose \(E \colon y^2 = f(x)\text{,}\) then the basis for

\begin{equation*} H^1_{\dR}(E) = H^1_{MW}(E')^- = \left\{ \frac{\diff x}{y},\frac{x\diff x}{y}\right\} \end{equation*}

moreover \(H^1_{\dR}(E) = H^0(E, \Omega ^1) \oplus U\) with \(U\) the unit root subspace. Compute the matrix of \(p\)-power frobenius \(F\text{,}\) want to find a basis for \(U\text{.}\) We know that

\begin{equation*} F\left(\frac{\diff x}{y}\right) \in p H^1_{\dR}(E) \end{equation*}

so that \(\mod {p^n}\text{,}\) \(U\) is the span of \(F^n \left(\frac{x\diff x}{y}\right)\) so for each \(n\) write

\begin{equation*} F^n\left(\frac{x\diff x}{y}\right) = a_n \frac{\diff x}{y} + b_n \frac{x\diff x}{y} \end{equation*}

then \(E_2(E,\omega ) = -12 \frac{a_n}{b_n} \pmod{p^n}\text{.}\)
