Remark 2.40.
  1. Let \(X\) be a genus 3 smooth plane quartic, say \(X = X_s(13)\text{,}\) the split Cartan curve of level 13. Then \(\dim H^1_\text{rig}(X) = 6\text{,}\) but \(\dim H^1_\text{rig}(U) = 45 = 6 + 39\text{,}\) where \(39 = 3 \deg r(x)\text{.}\)
  2. For applications to Coleman integrals between “good points”, proceed as before
    \begin{equation*} \int_{\phi(P)}^{\phi(Q)} \omega_i = \int_P^Q \phi^\ast \omega_i \end{equation*}
    and correct endpoints.
  3. For Coleman integration from a very bad point (a point above \(\infty\) or a point with \(x\)-coordinate such that \(r(x) = 0\)) \(B\text{,}\) split up the integral
    \begin{equation*} \int_B^Q \omega_i = \int_B^{B'} \omega_i + \int_{B'}^Q \omega_i \end{equation*}
    for \(B'\) a point near the boundary of the residue disk of \(B\text{.}\)
