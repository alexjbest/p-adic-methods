Now for various flavours of \(\pi _1(X)\) we have

\begin{equation*} \pi _1(X)_1 = \pi _1(X) ^{ab} = H_1(X) \end{equation*}

so above we should think of \(H_1\) as the abelianization of \(\pi _1\text{.}\) We can therefore try to replace it by \(\pi _1(X)_n\) for \(n \gt 1\text{.}\) We'll do this for the two homology groups associated to \(X\)

  1. \(p\)-adic étale homology
  2. de Rham homology

we can define the geometric étale fundamental group \(\pi _1^\et (X_{\overline K})\) such that

\begin{equation*} \pi_1^\et(X_{\overline K})^{ab} \simeq H_1^\et(X_{\overline K} , \widehat \ZZ) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \pi_1^\et(X_{\overline K})^{ab} \simeq H_1^\et(X_{\overline K} , \widehat \ZZ) \twoheadrightarrow H_1^\et(X_{\overline K}, \ZZ_p) \subseteq H_1^\et(X_{\overline K}, \QQ_p) = V = \GG_a^{2g}(\QQ_p) \end{equation*}

with action of \(G_K\) in particular \(G_K\) acts via \(\QQ_p\)-linear automorphisms on \(\GG_a^{2g}\text{.}\)
