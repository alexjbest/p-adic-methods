A unipotent isocrystal on \(\bar A\) is a \(A^\dagger\)-module \(M\) together with an (integrable) connection.

\begin{equation*} \nabla \colon \to M \otimes _{A^\dagger} \Omega ^1 (\otimes K) \end{equation*}

that is an iterated extension of trivial connections where a trivial connection is

\begin{equation*} \mathbf 1 = (A^\dagger, \diff)\text{.} \end{equation*}

A morphism of unipotent isocrystals is a map of \(A^\dagger\)-modules, that is horizontal, i.e. commutes with connection. We denote the category of unipotent isocrystals as \(Un(\bar A)\text{.}\) By Berthelot it only depends on \(\bar A\) as the notation suggests (in particular it doesn't depend on \(A^\dagger\text{.}\)
