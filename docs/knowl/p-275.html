Bloch and Kato defined \(\im \beta \) using \(p\)-adic Hodge theory and defined \(\Sel_{\QQ_p} (J)\) in terms of data from \(V\text{.}\)

  1. They wanted to extend Selmer and Shafarevich-Tate groups to motives beyond \(H^1\) of abelian varieties.
  2. this construction will let us extend the idea of Selmer group from
    \begin{equation*} T = H_1^\et(X_{\overline K}, \ZZ_p) \simeq \pi_1^\et(X_{\overline K})^\ab \otimes \ZZ_p \end{equation*}
    to certain (non-abelian) quotients of \(\pi_1^\et\text{.}\)
