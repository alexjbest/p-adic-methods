Choose a set \(\omega _0, \ldots, \omega _{2g + d -2} \in H^0(Y_{\QQ}, \Omega ^1)\) s.t.

  1. The differentials \(\omega _0, \ldots, \omega _{2g-1}\) are of the second kind on \(X\) and form a symplectic basis for \(H^1_{\dR}(X_\QQ)\) s.t. the cup product is the standard symplectic form with respect to this basis.
  2. The differentials
    \begin{equation*} \omega _{2g}, \ldots, \omega _{2g + d- 2} \end{equation*}
    are of the third kind on \(X\) (i.e. all poles are of order 1).

Let \(V_{\dR}(Y) = H^1_{\dR}(Y)^*\) and

\begin{equation*} T_0, \ldots, T_{2g +d-2} \end{equation*}

be the dual basis. Let

\begin{equation*} A_n^\dR = A_n^\dR(b) \end{equation*}

be the universal \(n\)-step unipotent object associated to \(\pi _1^\dR(X, b)\)-representation \(A_n^\dR(b)\) this vector bundle carries a Hodge filtration. For our applications to computing \(p\)-adic heights, take \(n=2\) and let \(A_Z = A_Z(b)\) be a certain quotient of \(A_2^\dR\text{.}\) We will compute the Hodge filtration on \(A_Z\) via characterization of the Hodge filtration universal property by Hadian.
