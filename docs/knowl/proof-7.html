Proof

Sketch: Recall the Kummer sequence

\begin{equation*} 0 \to \mu _n \to \GG_m \to \GG_m \to 0 \end{equation*}

we also have

\begin{equation*} H^0_\et(X , \GG _m) = k^\times \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} H^1_\et(X , \GG _m) = \Pic(X) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} H^q_\et(X , \GG _m) = 0 \text{ for }q \ge 2 \end{equation*}

take cohomology of the Kummer sequence to get

\begin{equation*} 0 \to H^0_\et(X_{\overline k} , \mu _n) \to k^\times \to k^\times \to 0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} 0 \to H^1_\et(X_{\overline k} , \mu _n) \to \Pic(X) \to H^2_\et(x, \mu _n) \to 0 \end{equation*}

We also have

\begin{equation*} 0 \to \Pic^0(X) \to \Pic(X) \to \ZZ \to 0 \end{equation*}

and we can identify \(\Pic^0(X)\) with the group of \(k\)-rational points of \(J\) of \(X\text{.}\) Note that multiplication by \(n\) is surjective and the kernel is \(\Pic^0\lb n\rb \text{.}\)
