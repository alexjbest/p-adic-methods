If \(Y\) is a smooth variety over \(\QQ_p\) then \(p\)-adic height gives for

\begin{equation*} V = H^i_\et(Y_{\overline \QQ_p}, \QQ_p) \end{equation*}

that

\begin{equation*} D_\dR(V) \xrightarrow\sim H^i_\dR(Y)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Respecting the Hodge filtration on either side. Also \(D_\dR^+(V)\) is naturally identified with \(F^0 D_\dR(V)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} F^m D_\dR(V) = (F^{m} B_\dR \otimes V)^{G_{K_v}} \end{equation*}

Frobenius on \(B_\crys\) induces a Frobenius map on \(D_\crys (V) \) and \(V = H^i_\et(Y_{\overline \QQ_p}, \QQ_p)\) and \(Y\) good reduction with special fibre \(Y_{\FF_p}\text{.}\) Then there's a natural isomorphism

\begin{equation*} D_\crys(V) \xrightarrow\sim H^1_\crys (Y_{\FF_p}, \QQ_p) \end{equation*}

respects frob on each side.
