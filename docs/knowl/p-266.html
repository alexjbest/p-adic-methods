Recall the Kummer exact sequence

\begin{equation*} 0 \to J[m] \to J \xrightarrow{\cdot m} J \to 0 \end{equation*}

take \(G_K = \absgal K\)-cohomology to get a long exact sequence which leads to a short exact sequence

\begin{equation*} 0 \to J(K)/mJ(K) \xrightarrow{\kappa_m } H^1(G_K, J[m]) \to H^1(G_K, J)[m] \to 0 \end{equation*}

where \(\kappa \) is the Kummer map. There is a canonical \(G_K\)-equivariant isomorphism

\begin{equation*} J[m] \simeq H_1^\et(J_{\overline K}, \ZZ/m) \simeq H_1^\et(X_{\overline K}, \ZZ/m)\text{.} \end{equation*}

The Kummer map gives an embedding

\begin{equation*} J(K)/mJ(K) \hookrightarrow H^1(G_k, H^\et_1(X_{\overline K}, \ZZ/m)) \end{equation*}

to get an embedding of \(J(K)\) rather than just \(J(K)/mJ(K)\) take \(m=p^n\) and inverse limit.
