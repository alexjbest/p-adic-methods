We want to start by removing \(J(K)\) from the diagram. Recall: Let \(M\) be an abelian group. The \(p\)-adic completion

\begin{equation*} \widehat M = \varprojlim_n M/p^nM \end{equation*}

is a \(\ZZ_p\)-module. We can get a \(\QQ_p\)-vector space by inverting \(p\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \widehat M [1/p]\simeq \widehat M\otimes_{\ZZ_p} \QQ_p\text{.} \end{equation*}

The group \(J(K_p)\) is compact so that the images of \(p^nJ(K_p)\) in \(\Lie J(K_p) \to 0\text{,}\) \(p\)-adically as \(n\to \infty \text{.}\) So the homomorphism

\begin{equation*} J(K_p) \to \Lie J_{K_p} \end{equation*}

factors through \(\widehat {J(K_p)}\) and hence also through \(\widehat{J(K_p)} \lb 1/p\rb \text{.}\) Since \(\log \) is a local diffeomorphism with finite kernel get a \(\QQ_p\)-linear map

\begin{equation*} \widehat{J(K_p)} [1/p] \to \Lie J_{K_p} \end{equation*}

is an isomorphism.
