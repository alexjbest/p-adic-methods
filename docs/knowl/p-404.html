Let \(G\) be a profinite group \(U\) a \(G\)-group. Let \(V\) be a continuous representation of \(G\) with an equivariant action of \(U\text{.}\) Then for any \(G\)-equivariant \(U\)-torsor \(P\) and also \(U\) acts on \(V\) on the left. We form the twist of \(V\) by \(P\) as follows, let \(\sim\) denote the equivalence.

\begin{equation*} (p,v) \sim (p\cdot u, u\inv v) \end{equation*}

on \(P\times V\) for all \(u \in U\text{.}\) Then the twist of \(V\) by \(P\)

\begin{equation*} V^{(P)} = P\times_U V / \sim \end{equation*}

has a continuous \(G\)-representation structure.
