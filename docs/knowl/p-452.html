There is an action of frobenius on path torsors

\begin{equation*} \pi _1^{rig}(\mathcal X_{\FF_p}, \bar b, x) \end{equation*}

of \({\pi_1^{rig}}\text{,}\) and \(n\)-step unipotent quotients of \(\pi _1^\rig\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \phi _n \colon A_n(\bar b, \bar x) \to A_n(\bar b, \bar x) \end{equation*}

there is a frobenius structure on \(\mathcal A_n ^\rig(\bar b)\) the universal \(n\)-step object. The frobenius structure is an isomorphism

\begin{equation*} \Phi _n \colon \phi ^* \mathcal A_n^\rig(\bar b) \xrightarrow \sim \mathcal A_n^\rig(b) \end{equation*}

of overconvergent unipotent isocrystals. There is a corresponding de Rham realization, the pull back of frobenius to \(\mathcal X_{\FF_p}\) gives frobenius on

\begin{equation*} \pi _1^\dR(X_{\QQ_p}, b, x) \end{equation*}

and a frobenius operator on the \(n\)-step unipotent quotients

\begin{equation*} \phi _n(b,x) \colon A_n^\dR (b, x) \to A_n^\dR(b,x)\text{.} \end{equation*}
