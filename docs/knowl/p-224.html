Recall: \(H^1_{\dR}(X)\) has a canonical non-degenerate form given by cup-product pairing

\begin{equation*} H^1_{\dR} (X/K) \times H^1_{\dR}(X/K) \to K \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} ([\mu _1],[\mu _2]) \mapsto [\mu _1] \cup [\mu _2] \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} [\mu _1] \cup [\mu _2] = \sum_Q \Res_Q\left( \mu _2\int \mu _1\right) \end{equation*}

note that \(\mu _1,\mu _2\) are differentials of the second kind (residue 0). So the residue does not depend on the choice of local integral for \(\mu _1\) since \(\mu _2\) has no residue at any point.
