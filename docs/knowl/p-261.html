Recall the classical Chabauty-Coleman diagram

\begin{equation*} \xymatrix{ X(K) \ar[r] \ar[d] & X(K_p)\ar[d] & \\ J(K) \ar[r] & J(K_p)\ar[r] & \Lie J_{K_p} } \end{equation*}

in the classical chabauty-coleman method, the image of \(J(K)\) in the \(g\)-dimensional space \(\Lie J_{K_p}\) spans a \(K_p\) subspace of dimension at most \(r = \rk J(K)\text{.}\) So if \(r \lt g\) there exists a non-zero \(K_p\) valued functional on \(\Lie J_{K_p}\) vanishing on \(J(K)\text{.}\) This pulls back to \(d\) non-zero locally analytic functions on \(X(K_p)\) that vanish on \(X(K)\text{.}\)
